Intake Interview Preparation

Get your questions answered.
Make your best contribution.

This takes about ten minutes. At the end you'll have a one-page prep sheet to bring into your intake conversation — what you know, what you need, and what you want to say.

◆
Your answers stay with you. What you write here doesn't go to your supervisor or HR. Your prep sheet belongs to you — share what you choose in the conversation, nothing more.
Part 1 of 4

What you bring

Knowledge and experience that deserves to be heard — including things that might not come up if nobody asks.

Question 1
Think about the work you did before this acquisition. What did you figure out — a practice, a habit, a shortcut, a fix — that worked better than how it's described in any manual?
It doesn't have to be dramatic. The best answers are usually specific and ordinary.
Question 2
Is there a safety practice from your previous work that you think is worth making sure Rensa knows about?
Near-misses, close calls, things that could go wrong — this is the right place to put them.
Question 3
If you could improve one specific thing about how work gets done in your area — and you knew it would actually be acted on — what would it be?
One thing. Be as specific as you can.
Part 2 of 4

What you need to know

The intake conversation is designed to answer your questions. These help you figure out which ones matter most.

Question 4
What's the most important thing you still don't understand about your role at Rensa?
Question 5
What's the specific question you most want to walk out of the intake interview having answered?
Write it the way you'd actually ask it.
Part 3 of 4

The things worth naming

Honest answers here make the intake conversation more useful — for you and for Rensa.

Question 6
What's been the hardest part of the transition so far?
Question 7
Is there something from your previous company you miss — a way of working, a practice, a habit — that you think genuinely mattered?
Not nostalgia. What actually worked that isn't here yet.
Part 4 of 4

Where you want to go

What you want from this chapter — honestly, not what you think you're supposed to want.

Question 8
What would make your first year at Rensa a genuine success — for you personally?
Question 9
Is there a skill or area you want to develop — that you think Rensa could actually help you build?
Question 10
What's one thing you'd want your interviewer to understand about you that probably won't come up on its own?
This is the thing you'd want said if you weren't in the room.
